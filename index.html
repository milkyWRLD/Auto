Specifications

Prelude V BB 2.2 i 16V

Engine

The 2.2L engine, the F22B, was used only in the domestic market. The initial Xi version received a variant with 130 hp at 5,300 rpm and 188 Nm of torque at 4,200 rpm. The Si version was equipped with a DOCH motor with an output of 160 hp and 215 Nm at 6,500 and 6,000 rpm, respectively.

Performance

The weakest Prelude can reach 200 km/h and accelerate to 100 km/h in just over 9 seconds. Stronger cars can reach almost 230 km/h, and acceleration to 100 km/h takes about 7 seconds. Average fuel consumption, according to the manufacturer, is about 8 liters per 100 km in the mixed cycle.

Running gear

The car has both independent double wishbone suspensions. The Type S and Type SH versions have a revised camber at the front. In addition, the improved versions are equipped with the 4WS system, which provides rear-wheel steering.

Information

It was produced from 1996 to 2001. The h22a engine power was raised to 220 hp for the first time in this body.

Service

The engines are reliable. Their service life is 200-300 thousand km and more. Due to high oil consumption, its level should be regularly checked. It is recommended to replace it every 10 thousand km, or twice as often with active driving. The spark plugs should be replaced every 10-15 km. The timing belt should be replaced at an interval of 100 thousand km.

The fifth generation proved to be a lesser seller. This was primarily due to internal competition from the more solid Accord sixth coupe and the much cheaper Civic seventh coupe. On the secondary market, the Honda Prelude-5 can be purchased for less than 200 thousand rubles. The cost of the most expensive variants exceeds 400 thousand rubles.
